Why they exist

A habitat for code-born camouflage.

01

One canvas. One law.

Each token is drawn on a 72×72 grid, nearest-neighbor scaled to display size. Black 1px outlines. Side-profile so you always see the turret eye — the CamoBit signature.

02

No layers folder.

There is no Photoshop stack. Pure procedures paint ellipses, tails, tongues, vines, and grounds. Same rules → same bit. Forever.

03

They breathe slowly.

Four frames: open · blink · open · look. Biological eyes blink once. Shades and lasers never double-blink — one eye, one cover, one beam.

04

Habitat, not hype props.

Grounds, plants, and extras are jungle-native: moss, lily pads, hanging vines, crickets, dew. Rarity is in the weights — not random stickers.

How rarity shows up in the image

  1. Trait odds — scarcer paints roll less often (eyes, body, sky, ground…).
  2. Stack score — rarer traits add up; the total ranks the bit.
  3. Tiers — score maps to Mythic / Legendary / Epic / Rare / Uncommon.
  4. Looks matter — top stacks can unlock gradient skies so rare feels rare, not just labeled.
  5. No twins — full trait DNA is unique across the whole supply.
